# Quick Guide: Searching by Abbreviations on WOL

The **wol.jw.org** site understands abbreviations to take you directly to a document, without the need to manually navigate through menus.

## The Basic Rule

Type in the search bar using exactly this structure:
**[Abbreviation] + [Space] + [Number]**

* **Abbreviation:** The official abbreviation of the book, brochure, or magazine (e.g., `lff`, `w`).
* **Number:** The chapter, lesson, or page you want to open.

---

## Practical Examples

### Books and Brochures
Just type the abbreviation followed by the number:
* **lff 5** (Opens the book *Enjoy Life Forever!*, lesson 5)
* **rq 12** (Opens the brochure *What Does God Require of Us?*, page 12)
* **bh 3** (Opens the book *What Does the Bible Really Teach?*, chapter 3)

### Magazines (The Watchtower and Awake!)
The rule combines the magazine letter with the last two digits of the year:
* **w24** (Brings up *The Watchtower* issues from the year 2024)
* **g23** (Brings up *Awake!* issues from the year 2023)
* **w15 15/2 4** (*The Watchtower* of 2015, February 15 issue, page 4)

---

## Important Tips

* **Don't worry about capitalization:** Typing `LFF 5` or `lff 5` gives the same result.
* **Direct access:** When searching for a valid abbreviation, the site creates a clickable link right at the top of the results taking you straight to the content, rather than just hunting for the word within the texts.
